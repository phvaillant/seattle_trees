About

This website has been developed to better explore the Seattle Tree dataset from the City of Seattle, while enriching the data.

Some data can seem odd - admire the two trees in the Puget Sound - but this map is also designed to help city officials enhance their own data. You can check here what the officials of SDOT were saying about the data back in 2012.

Maps (such as the seattle tree map and the SDOT tree map) have already been made in order to visualize the data, but I think that this map makes the exploration of the data easier and more enjoyable.

I am a PhD student at the Urban Ecology Lab at UW interested in data visualization and the properties of urban tree covers in cities. I believe in the benefits of a better understanding of species distribution, population trends, etc.
